Project: ReycleSmart Responsive Site

Challenge

RecyleSmart, a waste removal organization in Contra Costa County, California, hired MIG to rethink their website, and develop a print and digital newsletter. Through research provided to us by the organization, it became clear that a big challenge in developing this product would be reaching RecycleSmart's three distinct audiences, while also retaining one cohesive and accessible site.

Process

In analyzing the research RecycleSmart provided, in additon to the business goals that they laid out, a pain point for users became apparent. Although residents of Contra Costa are regularly recycling and using food-scrap waste bins, they are not aware of all the materials that can go into these two bins, making education and easy access to bin information for each audience a priority of the site. As a result of these findings, when developing wireframes, under each audience menu, I included clear links to bin information.

In approaching the site, I implemented an iterative process of sketches, wireframes, and mockups, all of which were tested in UXPin. Each iteration was tested with approximately three to five people, leading to a better understanding of navigation placement and content, use of the site's value proposition, and overall experience.

The site's hero graphic features a looping video, that shows three sequences. In addition to establishing an atmosphere for the site, the sequences visually depict each audience type accurately recycling and composting. After a writer developed concepts for the three scenarios, I was on set to art direct both the video and a still shoot.

For brand consistency, the site's visual design was influenced by the printed newsletter, which was completed prior to starting the the site redesign. The design implements the same sophisticated design techniques, use of layers and textures, and approachable and conversational language.
