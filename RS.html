Project: Responsive Site

Client: RecycleSmart

RecyleSmart, a waste removal organization in Contra Costa County, California, hired MIG to rethink their website, and develop a printed newsletter for their residential, single-family home users.

I began the site by analyzing research RecycleSmart provided us with on their three user types: those who live in single-family homes, those who live in apartments and condos, and commercial building owners and workers. Users are divided into these three categories based on the services and programs offered to the building types.

Reading through this data provided a better understanding of what RecycleSmart is trying to improve upon—increasing composting among single-family homes, and increasing recycling in apartments and condos, and in commercial buildings. The research also showed where the larger problem exists. Although residents of Contra Costa are regularly recycling and using food-scrap waste bins, they are not aware of all the materials that can go into these two bins, making education and easy access to bin information for each audience a priority of the site.

With this in mind, I started with a series of sketches and early wireframes that led to more refined mockups, all of which were tested in UXPin, providing a better understanding of navigation placement and content, use of the site's value proposition, and overall epxerience.

The site's hero graphic features a looping video, showing three sequences, to both create an atmosphere for the user, and visually depict the three audiences accurately recycling and composting. After a writer concepted the three scenarios, I was on set to art direct both the video and a still shoot.

For brand consistency, the site's visual design was influenced by the printed newsletter, which was completed prior to starting the the site redesign. The design implements the same sophisticated design techniques, use of layers and textures, and approachable and conversational language.
