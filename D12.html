WORK EXPERIENCE: MIG, Inc.

CLIENT: Caltrans

CHALLENGE:

Caltrans, the California Department of Transportation, was interested in building a site for District 12 in Orange County to educate the public on a study taking place to manage traffic and keep freeways moving.

In disucssing the content for the site, it became clear that it was necessary to explain to users what managed lanes are and why they are being considered for the area. Because of this, we came to the conclusion that a homepage that leads users through a succinct question and answer format, would help them have a better overall understanding. Then, through the interior pages, users can learn more about the specific process and study taking place in Orange County itself.

To further inform and educate users about what Caltrans was researching, two prominent buttons for a resource and video libary are at the top of the page.

In addition to leading the visual design for this site, I participated in the entire process from sketches to editing code. And, to further support community education and outreach for the Caltrans brand, I developed a fact sheet for events, and proposed updated logo designs.
