Project: Newsletter

Client: RecycleSmart

RecyleSmart, a waste removal organization in Contra Costa County, California, hired MIG to develop a printed newsletter design for their residential, single-family home users. Open to a new design and format, they wanted a look and feel that was refreshing and refined, and unlike other public organization newsletters. With all this in mind, I led the creative on the design.

I began the newsletter researching not only similiar pieces, but also broadened my observations to other editorial materials that engage and delight readers, specifically magazine publications. I studied various magazines, like Bon Appetit and Esquire, and found commonalities in the use of color, imagery, typography, language, and storytelling. I noticed the use of layers and patterns that the designs use to draw the reader in and create a feeling of sophistication and weight. Headlines and captions are conversational to reach readers on a personal level. Further, stories take on many different forms and sizes, from the highly visual to the shorter almost sentence long exclamations. After mapping all these conclusions on a mood board, I began to think about this newsletter in a similar way.

Collaborating with a writer and my Creative Director, we developed stories and infographics that would speak to the readers in new ways. We developed an interactive panel for children, teaching them about recyling and composting, while also highlighting everyday neighbors who are improving their community in interesting ways through waste removal.

With this newsletter I saw a project from concept to finished design, and was able to find a way to solve our client's goals and make them feel excited to send this piece out to their users.
